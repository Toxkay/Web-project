Spaghetti Carbonara

Course: Main Course

Description: A classic Italian pasta dish.

Ingredients (Serves 2–3)

  • 200g (7 oz) spaghetti
  • 100g (3.5 oz) pancetta or guanciale (cut into small cubes)
  • 2 large eggs
  • 1/2 cup (50g) grated Pecorino Romano or Parmesan cheese
  • 1 clove garlic (optional)
  • Freshly ground black pepper
  • Salt (for pasta water)

Materials Needed

  • Large pot (for boiling pasta)
  • Skillet or frying pan
  • Mixing bowl
  • Tongs or pasta fork
  • Grater (for cheese)
  • Whisk or fork (for mixing eggs and cheese)
  • Ladle or mug (to reserve pasta water)
  • Colander (optional)

Instructions

  1. Bring a large pot of salted water to a boil. Cook the spaghetti until al dente, according to the package instructions.
  2. In a bowl, whisk together the eggs and grated cheese until smooth. Add a generous amount of black pepper
  3. In a skillet over medium heat, cook the pancetta or guanciale until crispy. Optionally, add a smashed clove of garlic to the pan for aroma and remove it afterward.
  4. Once the pasta is ready, reserve about 1 cup of the pasta water, then drain the pasta. Add the pasta directly into the skillet with the pancetta (heat off or very low) and toss.
  5. Remove the pan from heat completely, then quickly pour in the egg and cheese mixture while tossing or stirring vigorously. Add a bit of reserved pasta water gradually until you get a smooth, creamy sauce (no scrambling!).
  6. Plate the pasta and top with extra cheese and black pepper.

Tips

  • Use freshly grated cheese for best flavor.
  • Don't scramble the eggs—combine off the heat.

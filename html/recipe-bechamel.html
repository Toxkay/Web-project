Béchamel Sauce

Course: Main Course

Description:Béchamel is a rich, pale-colored sauce made with milk.

Ingredients

  • 2 tablespoons butter
  • 2 tablespoons all-purpose flour
  • 2 cups milk (whole or 2%)
  • Salt to taste
  • 1/4 teaspoon ground white pepper (optional)
  • 1/4 teaspoon ground nutmeg (optional)

Materials Needed

  • Medium saucepan
  • Whisk
  • Measuring spoons
  • Measuring cups
  • Ladle

Instructions

  1. In a medium saucepan, melt butter over medium heat.
  2. Once the butter is melted, add flour and whisk continuously for 1-2 minutes to form a smooth paste (this is called a roux).
  3. Slowly add the milk while continuing to whisk, making sure no lumps form. Keep whisking until the sauce thickens (about 5-7 minutes).
  4. Once thickened, add salt, pepper, and nutmeg (if using), and whisk to combine.
  5. Remove from heat and use immediately or keep warm until ready to serve.

Tips

  • Use freshly grated cheese for best flavor.
  • Don't scramble the eggs—combine off the heat.
